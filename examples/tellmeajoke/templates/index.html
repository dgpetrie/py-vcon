Introduction to vCons

What's a vCon? It's like a PDF for conversations. A vCon is designed to contain, store and share conversations. vCons are created by communication systems to capture all of the important parts of a conversation: the recordings, the people, the "semantics" of the conversation, and the context of the conversation. vCons are designed to be shared between communication services, to power artificial intelligence, but most importantly, to enable tools that confirm the contents and the authenticity of human biometrics as we record them in customer conversations.

There are four sections in a vCon. a vCon has a dialog section to hold the recordings, a parties section to identify the people involved, an analysis section to hold the semantics of the conversation, and a context section to hold the context of the conversation. and an attachment section to carry related files (the context).

So, what's the hack?

We are building the standard vCon library. But we are still missing an important piece - the hello world vCon. Can you help us build it? It's a long tradition in signal processing to have a common sample.

We would like you to think of a joke, and record it here on the page, and submit it. We will then use your joke to build the hello world vCon, post it here, and to send it to the judges (and to the world). During the judging process, we will pick the funniest vCon and use it as the hello world vCon. A story you can tell your grandchildren.

Instructions

  1. Think of the funniest joke you know that, if you win and your voice becomes the world vCon standard, won't haunt your older self.
  2. Record and upload your joke, get yourself a vCon! Make it a 'wav', 'mp3', or 'm4a' file, and short if you can.
  3. We will return a brand spanking new vCon, made from your joke. Frame it!
  4. Wait for the judges to pick the funniest joke.
  5. If you win, your voice will become the world vCon standard. And you buy dinner.
